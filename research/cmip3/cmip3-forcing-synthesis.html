Key Findings

  • GFDL CM2.0 and CM2.1 and GISS ModelE-H/R used fixed climatology for mineral dust (MD) and sea salt (SS) despite Table S1 recording Y (time-varying) for both; all four models carry * conflicts on MD and SS.
  • CCCma CGCM3.1 T47 and T63 applied fixed scalar values for BC, OC, MD, and SS — not time-varying aerosol datasets as implied by Table S1 — with concentrations held constant at 1850–2000 mean values throughout the 20C3M period.
  • CNRM-CM3 (1370 W m−2) and IPSL-CM4 (1365 W m−2) used a fixed solar constant rather than a time-varying reconstruction, conflicting with the Table S1 Y claim for SO.
  • MIUB ECHO-G applied flux corrections to both heat and freshwater fluxes (FXc*); Table S1 listed only F (freshwater), making the heat-flux correction an undocumented departure from the metadata record.
  • MIROC3.2 HiRes and MedRes treated MD and SS as time-varying but via SPRINTARS interactive aerosol diagnostics (TV~), not prescribed external datasets — a methodological distinction not captured in the original binary table.
  • MPI-ECHAM5 omitted BC, OC, and LU from 20C3M forcing despite Table S1 Y claims on all three, running a sulphate-only anthropogenic aerosol configuration; these three cells carry * conflicts.
