Key Findings
  • NCAR CCSM4 and all five CESM1 variants: Three simultaneous deviations — G uses IPCC TAR concentrations (not Meinshausen 2011), LU uses Hurtt 2006 (not the 2011 revision), VL uses Ammann 2003 stratospheric optical depths (not Sato 1993 updated). The CESM1-FASTCHEM and CESM1-WACCM branches add further complexity with internally generated chemistry.
  • IPSL IPSL-CM5A-LR/MR and CM5B-LR: Ozone and all aerosol fields (SD/SI/BC/OC/MD/SS) supplied by offline INCA/REPROBUS chemistry runs rather than the Cionni 2011 / Lamarque 2010 standard datasets directly; consistent deviation across all three model configurations.
  • MPI-M MPI-ESM-LR/MR/P family: Three deviations — aerosols from Kinne MACv1 global climatology (not Lamarque 2010 transient), solar from SPARC/SOLARIS TSI composite (not Wang 2005), volcanic from Stenchikov 1998 PADS stratospheric aerosol data (not Sato 1993 updated). All three ESM variants share identical forcing deviations.
  • MOHC HadGEM2-ES volcanic forcing: Sato 1993 extended with a Thomason 2008 background aerosol floor — unique deviation among UKMO models; HadGEM2-CC and HadGEM2-AO carry the same treatment. HadCM3 additionally uses older aerosol climatologies for direct effect.
  • CSIRO-BOM ACCESS1.0 and ACCESS1.3 land use: Explicitly omitted — "nor do we include prescribed land use change" (Dix et al. 2013) — marking LU as dev. All other forcing fields verified as standard, making these the cleanest single-deviation models in the ensemble.
  • BCC BCC-CSM1-1 and BCC-CSM1-1-m: Collins 2006 CAM3 fixed-climatology optical depths used for all aerosol components (SD/SI/BC/OC/MD/SS) — a single FXc aerosol climatology substituted across all six aerosol fields rather than the Lamarque 2010 transient emissions.
